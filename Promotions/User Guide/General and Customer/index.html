    Promotion Card

    Once you have created a new promotion, you can open the card and define the promotion settings.

    General FastTab

    This includes the number and brief description of the promotion, along with general rules for the promotion, such as: the date range that the promotion will be valid for (if left blank it will apply on all dates); if it applies to a specific currency code (blank means LCY – local currency, usually GB £); whether conversion from other currencies is allowed; whether benefit multiplication is allowed and whether or not the promotion is enabled.

    NB: If enabled is not ticked, the promotion will not be applied, even if the criteria are met. If you want to change / update the promotion, it must be disabled before you can make changes. Remember to enable the promotion again when you are ready.

    The Cannot Be Combined field allows the user to define that this promotion cannot be combined with others on the same document or on the same sales line.

    If this field is blank, any number of promotions can be applied at the same time to the sales order. If On Document is selected, promotions cannot be combined on the sales order. If On Line is selected, multiple promotions can be applied as long as they do not affect the same sales line.

    The Allow Benefit Multiplication option on the General FastTab determines whether the benefit of the promotion can be multiplied or not. For example, with a BOGOF promotion, if Allow Multiplication has been ticked, a customer that buys 3 of item X gets another 3 free. However, some other promotions may not wish to have this multiplication effect, for example in a "spend £100 and get £20 off" promotion the company may not want to give a £40 discount if the customer spends £200. In that case on the Promotion Card ‘Allow Promotion Benefit’ should be unticked

    Customer FastTab

    The customer tab is where you determine which customers are eligible for the promotion. You can include all customers, specific customer price groups, or individual customers.
