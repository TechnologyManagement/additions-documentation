    Accounts FastTab

    The next tab down is the Accounts FastTab, this is where you define how customers connect to the website as well as how certain payments made through the website will be managed.

    When a customer who isn’t logged in as registered user is browsing items on the web site, the prices they are shown are the based on the pricing for the Customer No. stated in the Default Customer for Pricing field. When a customer is registered correctly on the web site they will then see the sales prices specific to their Microsoft Dynamics NAV user account.

    NB: If the default customer does not have any sales prices setup against them then to get the price we will look to the item card.

    The Default Salesperson is applied to sales orders/return orders when they arrive in Microsoft Dynamics NAV as a pending inbound document. This can be left blank if no default sales person is required.

    This is sometimes used to identify orders made by the website versus orders made manually through Microsoft Dynamics NAV

    Web Site customers can use the My Account Portal to view their invoice/return order history. The Display History Period is a date formula determining how far back in time documents are shown. E.g. 6M with show the previous 6 months of invoices.

    The Web User Approval Required field is ticked as part of the default configuration and means that new users that register on the website need to be approved before they can place orders.

    As part of My Account Portal (separate user guide). Your customers can view invoices and query those invoices should they want to. The On-hold code for Transaction Queries field contains text that appears next to the ledger entries in Microsoft Dynamics NAV, of the invoice that is being queried by the customer. It is set as WEB as part of default configuration, however this can be changed to be something more meaningful to your business requirements.

    For every customer that registers to your website you must create them as a new Microsoft Dynamics NAV user. To do this you must tick New Customer Per Reg. User. This will not happen if the customer on validates their Microsoft Dynamics NAV account at the registration stage.

    The Web Sales Customer No. field only needs to be populated with a default customer, when a temporary customer record is created it uses this account to link the contact to. Once your customers order has been accepted then they will be linked to a newly created customer card of their own.

    NB: If your customers has registered themselves with a valid NAV customer linked to their account the above will not apply to them.

    Through the My Account Portal aspect of the website your customers can pay outstanding invoices. These payments are automatically posted to the general ledger, a document number is required for the entries in NAV. The Payment no. series determines the document numbers used.

    You will also need to fill in the Payment Balancing Acc. Type which is the type of account the payment is being balanced against. This will most likely be a Bank Account. And the Payment Balancing Acc. No., which is the account the payment is posted to. Select the bank account that cash receipts from web orders will be posted to.
